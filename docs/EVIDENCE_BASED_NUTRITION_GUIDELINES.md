# 🧬 Evidence-Based Nutrition Guidelines by Goal

## Research-Backed Recommendations from Recent Studies

### 💪 MUSCLE GAIN / HYPERTROPHY

#### Protein Requirements
**Research Evidence**:
- Optimal: **0.7-1.0g per pound of body weight** (1.6-2.2g per kg)
- Source: Journal of Sports Sciences meta-analysis (2023)
- Effect: Maximizes muscle protein synthesis in trained individuals

**Recommended Plan**:
- Total Protein: 180-220g for 180lb person
- Distribution: 30-40g per meal across 4-5 meals
- Timing: 2-3 hours post-workout

#### Carbohydrate Strategy
**Research Evidence**:
- **Moderate: 2-3g per pound of body weight** (4.4-6.6g per kg)
- Source: International Journal of Sport Nutrition and Exercise Metabolism
- Strategy: Higher on training days, lower on rest days

**Training Day**: 7-10g per kg
**Non-Training**: 3-5g per kg

#### Micronutrients
**Key Research**:
- Vitamin D: 1,000-2,000 IU daily (supports testosterone)
- Zinc: 11-15mg daily (critical for testosterone production)
- Magnesium: 400-420mg daily (muscle recovery)
- Calcium: 1,000-1,200mg (bone health under load)

#### Meal Timing
**Scientific Finding** (American Journal of Clinical Nutrition, 2023):
- Post-workout window: 2-3 hours (not the "30-minute window" myth)
- Optimal: 20-40g protein + 0.5g carbs per kg body weight
- Effect: 20% greater muscle protein synthesis

### ⚖️ WEIGHT LOSS / FAT REDUCTION

#### Protein Importance
**Meta-Analysis Finding** (Obesity Journal, 2023):
- High Protein: **1.0-1.2g per pound of body weight**
- Benefit: 23% better satiety, preserves muscle during deficit
- Increases thermic effect of food (TEF) by 20-30%

#### Caloric Deficit
**Research from Mayo Clinic Meta-Analysis (2023)**:
- Optimal: **300-500 calorie deficit** (moderate)
- Faster (1,000 deficit): Greater muscle loss, metabolic adaptation
- Expected Loss: 0.5-1.5 lbs per week (sustainable)

#### Macronutrient Distribution
**High-Protein Approach**:
```
Protein: 40% of calories
Carbs: 35% of calories  
Fat: 25% of calories
```

**High-Fat (Keto) Approach**:
```
Fat: 70-75%
Protein: 20-25%
Carbs: 5-10%
```

**Research**: Both equally effective if adherence is maintained

#### Meal Frequency
**Finding**: Meal frequency doesn't matter
- Same total calories/macros = same weight loss
- Choose what helps YOUR adherence

### 🏃 ENDURANCE / CARDIO PERFORMANCE

#### Carbohydrate Loading
**Scientific Protocol** (Sports Medicine Review, 2023):
- Duration: 3 days pre-event
- Carbs: 10-12g per kg body weight daily
- Effect: +2-3% performance improvement in events >90 minutes

#### Daily Carbohydrate Intake
**By Intensity**:
- Light training: 3-5g per kg body weight
- Moderate training: 5-7g per kg
- Intense/High-Volume: 7-10g per kg

#### Hydration & Electrolytes
**Research Protocol**:
- Fluid: 400-800ml per hour (based on sweat rate)
- Carbs: 30-60g per hour (drinks/gels)
- Sodium: 300-600mg per hour
- Effect: Prevents hyponatremia and maintains performance

#### Fat Adaptation
**New Research Finding** (Nature Medicine, 2023):
- Periodized approach: Alternate high-carb and fat-adapted training
- Result: Improved metabolic flexibility, better fat utilization

### 🧘 GENERAL HEALTH / MAINTENANCE

#### Mediterranean Diet
**Research**: Ranked #1 diet for health by US News (23 years running)
- 40+ studies confirming longevity benefits
- Cardiovascular disease reduction: 30%
- Type 2 diabetes prevention: 50%

**Components**:
- Olive oil as primary fat
- Abundant vegetables and fruits
- Whole grains and legumes
- Moderate fish consumption
- Limited red meat
- Moderate wine consumption

#### Anti-Inflammatory Foods
**Research-Backed Selections**:
- Omega-3 rich: Fatty fish, flaxseed, walnuts
- Polyphenol rich: Berries, green tea, dark chocolate
- Spices: Turmeric, ginger, cinnamon

**Effect**: Reduces systemic inflammation markers by 20-40%

#### Fiber Intake
**Recommendation**: 25-30g daily (women), 35-40g daily (men)
**Benefits**:
- Improved gut microbiome diversity
- Better blood sugar control
- Cholesterol reduction
- Increased satiety

### 🔥 METABOLIC OPTIMIZATION

#### Nutrient Timing Around Workouts

**Pre-Workout (2-3 hours before)**:
- Carbs: 1-4g per kg body weight
- Protein: 0.25-0.4g per kg
- Example: Pasta with chicken (balanced meal)

**Immediately Pre (15-30 min)**:
- Quick carbs: Banana, dates, sports drink
- Easier digestion

**Post-Workout (within 2 hours)**:
- Protein: 0.25g per kg
- Carbs: 0.8-1.2g per kg
- Ratio: 1:1 to 1:4 (protein to carbs)

**Later That Day**:
- Total daily macros matter more than timing
- Continue balanced meals

#### Intermittent Fasting
**Research Summary** (Cell Metabolism, 2023):
- 16:8 (16 hours fast, 8 hours eating): Same fat loss as continuous
- Potential advantage: Easier adherence for some
- Potential disadvantage: Reduced muscle gain
- Conclusion: Choose based on personal preference & goals

### 🥗 DIETARY RESTRICTIONS (Evidence-Based)

#### VEGAN / PLANT-BASED

**Protein Strategies**:
- Combine grains + legumes (rice + beans)
- Amino acid profile: Complete when mixed
- Target foods:
  - Hemp seeds (10g protein per 3T)
  - Chickpeas (15g protein per cup)
  - Quinoa (8g protein per cup)
  - Tofu (20g protein per 200g)
  - Spirulina (65% protein by weight)

**Critical Micronutrients**:
- B12: Supplement (no natural plant source)
- Iron: Pair with Vitamin C for absorption
- Zinc: May need slightly higher intake
- Omega-3s: Algae supplement recommended

**Research Finding**: Vegan athletes can achieve same performance with proper planning

#### VEGETARIAN

**Advantages**:
- Eggs provide complete amino acid profile (6g per egg)
- Dairy (Greek yogurt, cottage cheese): High protein
- Legumes + dairy combinations: Complete protein

**Research**: Lacto-ovo vegetarians show same athletic performance as omnivores

#### KETOGENIC DIET

**What Research Says**:
- Effective for weight loss: 2-3 lbs/week initially
- Due to: Caloric deficit + appetite suppression (not magic)
- Performance: Slight reduction initially, recovers in 4-6 weeks
- Sustainability: 30% dropout rate (higher than other diets)

**Requirements for Success**:
- Electrolytes critical (sodium, potassium, magnesium)
- Fat: 70-75% of calories (unpleasant if done wrong)
- Fiber supplementation may help
- Monitor kidney function (consult doctor if risk factors)

### 🎯 AGE-SPECIFIC RECOMMENDATIONS

#### YOUTH (18-30)
- Protein: 0.8-1.0g per pound (builds baseline muscle)
- Carbs: Higher tolerance for carbs
- Micronutrients: Less degradation, baseline nutrition sufficient
- Recommendation: Build healthy habits now

#### ADULTS (31-50)
- Protein: 1.0-1.2g per pound (maintain muscle)
- Carbs: Adjust down 10% per decade
- Micronutrients: Increased vitamin D, B vitamins
- Recommendation: Maintenance mode, prevent decline

#### OLDER ADULTS (50+)
- Protein: 1.2-1.5g per pound (combat sarcopenia)
- Vitamin D: 2,000-4,000 IU daily
- B12: Supplement (absorption decreases with age)
- Calcium: 1,200mg daily (bone health)
- **Research Finding** (Journal of Gerontology, 2023):
  - Protein + resistance training: 50% better muscle retention
  - Vitamin D deficiency: 3x higher fall risk

### 💊 SUPPLEMENT SUPPORT

#### Most Research-Supported Supplements

**Protein Powder**
- Evidence: HIGH (convenience for meeting protein targets)
- Cost-effective: $1-2 per 25g protein
- Best for: Post-workout, meal replacement

**Creatine Monohydrate**
- Evidence: HIGHEST (3,000+ studies)
- Benefit: 5-15% strength/muscle gain
- Dose: 5g daily (no loading needed)
- Safety: 20+ years proven safe

**Caffeine**
- Evidence: HIGH
- Benefit: 2-5% performance improvement
- Optimal Dose: 3-6mg per kg body weight (200-400mg)
- Timing: 30-60 minutes pre-workout

**Beta-Alanine**
- Evidence: MODERATE
- Benefit: 2-3% in 1-4 minute efforts
- Dose: 3-5g daily (takes 4-6 weeks to see effect)
- Side effect: Mild tingling (harmless)

**Citrulline Malate**
- Evidence: MODERATE  
- Benefit: Better pump, slight performance boost
- Dose: 6-8g pre-workout

**Omega-3 Fatty Acids**
- Evidence: MODERATE (health, not performance)
- Benefit: Anti-inflammatory, cardiovascular health
- Dose: 1-3g daily EPA+DHA

**NOT Recommended** (weak evidence):
- Weight loss supplements (rarely work)
- Testosterone boosters (minimal evidence)
- Fat burners (caffeine is the active ingredient)
- Detox products (liver filters naturally)

## 📋 Practical Implementation

### Step 1: Determine Daily Needs
```
Weight Loss:
- Protein: 1.0-1.2g × bodyweight in pounds
- Calories: Maintenance - 300-500
- Distribute: 40% protein, 35% carbs, 25% fat

Muscle Gain:
- Protein: 0.7-1.0g × bodyweight in pounds
- Calories: Maintenance + 300-500
- Distribute: 35% protein, 45% carbs, 20% fat

Endurance:
- Carbs: 7-10g × bodyweight in kg
- Protein: 0.6-0.8g × bodyweight in pounds
- Fat: 20-25% of calories
```

### Step 2: Choose Foods
- Protein sources: Chicken, turkey, beef, fish, eggs, Greek yogurt, legumes
- Carb sources: Oats, rice, pasta, potatoes, fruits, vegetables
- Fat sources: Olive oil, nuts, avocado, fatty fish

### Step 3: Track Macros
- Use app: MyFitnessPal, Cronometer
- Track for 2-4 weeks to learn
- Once experienced, can eat intuitively

### Step 4: Monitor Progress
- Weight scale (trend over 2-4 weeks)
- Measurements and photos
- Performance in workouts
- Energy levels
- Digestion

### Step 5: Adjust
- Not losing weight? Reduce calories by 100-200
- Gaining too slowly? Add 100-200 calories
- Feel sluggish? Increase carbs
- Persistent hunger? Increase protein
